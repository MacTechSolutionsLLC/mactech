**Framework:** NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF)

---

## 1. Purpose

This document provides exact, assessor-safe language that MacTech Solutions may use when making claims about NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF) alignment in proposals, customer responses, and internal assurance statements.

**Critical Importance:** The language in this document has been carefully crafted to be:
- **Accurate:** Truthful and supportable by existing evidence
- **Defensible:** Able to withstand audit questioning
- **Conservative:** Avoids overstatement or false claims
- **Compliant:** Does not violate false statement laws (18 U.S.C. § 1001)

**Mandatory Compliance:** All personnel making RMF-related claims MUST use only the approved language from this document. Deviation from approved language is prohibited.

## 2. Approved Claim Language

### 2.1 Primary Alignment Statement

**Approved Primary Statement:**

"MacTech Solutions aligns its system security governance with the NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF)."

**Usage Context:** General purpose statement for proposals, marketing materials, and customer communications.

**Supporting Evidence:** This claim is supported by the RMF Alignment Package, which maps existing CMMC Level 2 controls and governance practices to RMF principles.

---

### 2.2 Detailed Alignment Statements

**Approved Detailed Statements:**

1. **"Our security practices are informed by RMF principles."**

2. **"Our system security governance is structured consistent with RMF."**

3. **"We follow RMF-aligned processes for risk management and security control implementation."**

4. **"MacTech Solutions maintains RMF alignment documentation that demonstrates how our existing security governance aligns with RMF principles."**

5. **"Our security governance addresses all six RMF steps: Categorize, Select, Implement, Assess, Authorize, and Monitor."**

**Usage Context:** Technical proposals, detailed customer responses, compliance documentation.

**Supporting Evidence:** See `rmf-alignment-overview.md` and other RMF alignment documents for detailed mappings.

---

### 2.3 RMF Step-Specific Statements

**Step 1 - Categorize:**

"MacTech Solutions' system categorization aligns with RMF Step 1 expectations through FIPS 199-style impact assessment, categorizing the system as Moderate impact level based on CUI handling requirements."

**Step 2 - Select:**

"MacTech Solutions' control selection aligns with RMF Step 2 expectations through NIST SP 800-171 Rev. 2 control baseline selection and risk-informed tailoring decisions."

**Step 3 - Implement:**

"MacTech Solutions' control implementation aligns with RMF Step 3 expectations through comprehensive implementation of 110 NIST SP 800-171 controls, with 81 controls implemented internally and 12 controls inherited from platform providers."

**Step 4 - Assess:**

"MacTech Solutions' security control assessment aligns conceptually with RMF Step 4 expectations through CMMC Level 2 self-assessment and automated compliance audit processes."

**Step 5 - Authorize:**

"MacTech Solutions' authorization decision context aligns conceptually with RMF Step 5 expectations through documented authorization boundary and decision framework, describing how authorization decisions would be supported if formal RMF authorization were pursued."

**Step 6 - Monitor:**

"MacTech Solutions' continuous monitoring aligns with RMF Step 6 expectations through comprehensive audit logging, vulnerability management, change management, and incident response processes."

**Usage Context:** Detailed technical discussions, RMF step-specific requirements in RFPs.

**Supporting Evidence:** See specific RMF alignment documents for step-by-step alignment details.

## 3. Explicit Prohibited Language (What NOT to Say)

### 3.1 Prohibited Authorization Claims

**DO NOT SAY:**
- ❌ "MacTech Solutions has an ATO"
- ❌ "We are authorized to operate"
- ❌ "MacTech Solutions holds an Authorization to Operate"
- ❌ "We have achieved ATO"
- ❌ "ATO granted"
- ❌ "Authorized to operate"

**Reason:** Authorization to Operate (ATO) requires a formal RMF authorization process through an Authorizing Official. MacTech Solutions has not pursued formal RMF authorization.

---

### 3.2 Prohibited RMF Authorization Claims

**DO NOT SAY:**
- ❌ "MacTech Solutions is RMF authorized"
- ❌ "We are RMF authorized"
- ❌ "MacTech Solutions holds RMF authorization"
- ❌ "RMF authorized"
- ❌ "Authorized under RMF"

**Reason:** RMF authorization requires a formal authorization process through an Authorizing Official. MacTech Solutions has not pursued formal RMF authorization.

---

### 3.3 Prohibited Approval Claims

**DO NOT SAY:**
- ❌ "MacTech Solutions is approved for operation"
- ❌ "We are approved to operate"
- ❌ "Approved for operation"
- ❌ "Approved under RMF"

**Reason:** Approval for operation requires formal authorization. MacTech Solutions has not pursued formal RMF authorization.

---

### 3.4 Prohibited Compliance Claims

**DO NOT SAY:**
- ❌ "MacTech Solutions is compliant with RMF"
- ❌ "We comply with RMF"
- ❌ "RMF compliant"
- ❌ "Compliant with NIST RMF"
- ❌ "Meets RMF requirements"

**Reason:** "Compliant with RMF" or "meets RMF requirements" implies formal authorization or assessment. Use "aligned with" or "structured consistent with" instead.

---

### 3.5 Prohibited Assessment Claims

**DO NOT SAY:**
- ❌ "RMF assessed"
- ❌ "RMF assessment completed"
- ❌ "Formal RMF assessment"
- ❌ "RMF evaluation completed"

**Reason:** Formal RMF assessment requires a formal assessment process. MacTech Solutions has not undergone formal RMF assessment.

## 4. Context-Specific Language

### 4.1 DoD Proposals

**Recommended Language for DoD Proposals:**

"MacTech Solutions maintains CMMC 2.0 Level 2 compliance as our primary cybersecurity framework. Additionally, our system security governance aligns with the NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF), demonstrating how our existing CMMC Level 2 controls and governance practices follow RMF-aligned processes for risk management and security control implementation."

**Rationale:** Emphasizes CMMC as primary (required for DoD), with RMF alignment as supplementary information.

**Supporting Documents:**
- CMMC Executive Attestation
- RMF Alignment Overview
- RMF Implementation Summary

---

### 4.2 Civilian Agency Proposals

**Recommended Language for Civilian Agency Proposals:**

"MacTech Solutions aligns its system security governance with the NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF), addressing all six RMF steps: Categorize (system categorization), Select (control baseline selection), Implement (control implementation), Assess (security control assessment), Authorize (authorization decision context), and Monitor (continuous monitoring). Our RMF alignment is based on our CMMC 2.0 Level 2 implementation, which provides comprehensive security controls and governance structures aligned with RMF principles."

**Rationale:** Emphasizes RMF alignment (often preferred by civilian agencies) while maintaining CMMC reference for credibility.

**Supporting Documents:**
- RMF Alignment Overview
- RMF System Categorization
- RMF Implementation Summary
- CMMC Executive Attestation (as supporting evidence)

---

### 4.3 Prime Contractor Responses

**Recommended Language for Prime Contractor Security Reviews:**

"MacTech Solutions maintains CMMC 2.0 Level 2 compliance and aligns its system security governance with the NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF). Our RMF Alignment Package documents how existing CMMC Level 2 controls and governance practices align with RMF principles, providing a comprehensive view of our security governance across both frameworks."

**Rationale:** Acknowledges both frameworks, showing comprehensive security governance approach.

**Supporting Documents:**
- CMMC Executive Attestation
- RMF Alignment Overview
- RMF Implementation Summary

---

### 4.4 RFP Responses

**Recommended Language for RFP Security Sections:**

"MacTech Solutions aligns its system security governance with the NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF), addressing all six RMF steps: Categorize (Moderate impact level system categorization), Select (NIST SP 800-171 Rev. 2 control baseline), Implement (110 controls with 97% readiness), Assess (conceptually aligned through CMMC self-assessment), Authorize (authorization decision context documented), and Monitor (continuous monitoring CONOPS). Our RMF alignment is based on our CMMC 2.0 Level 2 implementation."

**Rationale:** Provides specific RMF step details while maintaining accuracy and traceability.

**Supporting Documents:**
- RMF Alignment Overview
- RMF System Categorization
- RMF Implementation Summary (for detailed step coverage)

---

### 4.5 Customer Security Questionnaires

**Recommended Language for Security Questionnaires:**

"MacTech Solutions aligns its system security governance with the NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF). Our security practices are informed by RMF principles, and our system security governance is structured consistent with RMF. All six RMF steps are addressed through our existing CMMC Level 2 implementation. RMF alignment documentation is available for review upon request."

**Rationale:** Concise, accurate, and offers documentation for verification.

**Supporting Documents:**
- RMF Alignment Overview (primary reference)
- Offer to provide additional documentation if requested

## 6. How to Answer RMF Questions

### 6.1 "Do you have an ATO?"

**Approved Response:**

"We have not pursued formal RMF authorization, so we do not have an Authorization to Operate (ATO). However, our system security governance aligns with RMF principles, and our controls are structured consistent with RMF expectations. Our RMF alignment documentation is available for review."

---

### 6.2 "Are you RMF authorized?"

**Approved Response:**

"We are not formally RMF authorized. Our security practices are informed by RMF principles and our system governance follows RMF-aligned processes. We maintain RMF alignment documentation that demonstrates how our existing CMMC Level 2 controls align with RMF principles."

---

### 6.3 "Are you compliant with RMF?"

**Approved Response:**

"MacTech Solutions does not claim RMF compliance, as RMF compliance requires formal authorization. However, we align our system security governance with the NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF). Our security practices are informed by RMF principles, and our system security governance is structured consistent with RMF."

---

### 6.4 "Can you provide RMF documentation?"

**Approved Response:**

"MacTech Solutions maintains RMF alignment documentation that demonstrates how our existing controls and governance practices align with RMF principles. The RMF Alignment Package includes system categorization, control selection, implementation summary, risk assessment, authorization context, continuous monitoring, and alignment overview. The documentation is available for review."

---

### 6.5 "How does RMF relate to your CMMC compliance?"

**Approved Response:**

"MacTech Solutions' primary cybersecurity framework is CMMC 2.0 Level 2, which is required for DoD contracts. Our CMMC Level 2 implementation includes 110 NIST SP 800-171 controls and comprehensive security governance. We have documented how these existing CMMC controls and governance practices align with RMF principles in our RMF Alignment Package. This demonstrates that our CMMC implementation follows RMF-aligned processes across all six RMF steps."

---

### 6.6 "What is your RMF status?"

**Approved Response:**

"MacTech Solutions is not formally RMF authorized. We align our system security governance with the NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF). Our security practices are informed by RMF principles, and our system security governance is structured consistent with RMF. Our RMF alignment is based on existing CMMC Level 2 controls and governance practices and is documented in our RMF Alignment Package."

---

### 6.7 "Have you completed RMF Steps 1-6?"

**Approved Response:**

"MacTech Solutions has documented alignment with all six RMF steps through our existing CMMC Level 2 implementation. Steps 1 (Categorize), 2 (Select), 3 (Implement), and 6 (Monitor) are aligned operationally. Steps 4 (Assess) and 5 (Authorize) are aligned conceptually, describing how these steps would be supported if formal RMF authorization were pursued. Our RMF alignment documentation is available for review."

## 7. Legal and Compliance Considerations

### 7.1 False Statements Law (18 U.S.C. § 1001)

**Critical Reminder:**

Knowingly and willfully making a false, fictitious, or fraudulent statement or representation in connection with federal contracts is a violation of 18 U.S.C. § 1001 and may result in:
- Criminal penalties including fines and imprisonment
- Civil penalties
- Contract termination
- Debarment from federal contracting

**All claims in this document are:**
- Truthful and accurate
- Supportable by existing evidence
- Conservative and defensible
- Reviewed for legal compliance

---

### 7.2 Accuracy Requirements

**Mandatory Requirements:**

1. **Truthfulness:** All claims must be truthful and accurate
2. **Evidence Support:** All claims must be supportable by existing evidence
3. **No Speculation:** Do not make claims about future capabilities or planned improvements
4. **No Overstatement:** Use only approved language; do not embellish or exaggerate
5. **Documentation:** Maintain documentation supporting all claims

---

### 7.3 Review Process

**Before Using RMF Claims:**

1. Review this document for approved language
2. Verify that supporting evidence exists
3. Ensure claims are appropriate for the context
4. Consult compliance team if uncertain
5. Document any deviations (which should be rare)
