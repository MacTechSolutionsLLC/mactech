# SOC 2 Claim Language

**Document Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** 2026-01-25  
**Classification:** Internal Use  
**Framework:** AICPA Trust Services Criteria (2017, with 2022 revised points of focus)  
**Reference:** SOC 2 Type I - Security (Common Criteria)

**Applies to:** MacTech Solutions Application - CMMC 2.0 Level 2 System

---

## 1. Purpose

This document provides exact, assessor-safe language that MacTech Solutions may use when making claims about SOC 2 Type I readiness in proposals, customer responses, and internal assurance statements.

**Critical Importance:** The language in this document has been carefully crafted to be:
- **Accurate:** Truthful and supportable by existing evidence
- **Defensible:** Able to withstand audit questioning
- **Conservative:** Avoids overstatement or false claims
- **Compliant:** Does not violate false statement laws (18 U.S.C. § 1001)

**Mandatory Compliance:** All personnel making SOC 2-related claims MUST use only the approved language from this document. Deviation from approved language is prohibited.

## 3. Explicit Prohibited Language (What NOT to Say)

### 3.1 Prohibited Certification Claims

**DO NOT SAY:**
- ❌ "MacTech Solutions is SOC 2 certified"
- ❌ "We are SOC 2 certified"
- ❌ "MacTech Solutions holds SOC 2 certification"
- ❌ "We have achieved SOC 2 certification"
- ❌ "SOC 2 certified"
- ❌ "SOC 2 Type I certified"

**Reason:** SOC 2 does not have a certification program. SOC 2 provides examination and attestation reports, not certification. MacTech Solutions has not undergone a SOC 2 examination.

---

### 3.2 Prohibited Audit/Attestation Claims

**DO NOT SAY:**
- ❌ "MacTech Solutions is SOC 2 audited"
- ❌ "We are SOC 2 audited"
- ❌ "MacTech Solutions has been SOC 2 examined"
- ❌ "We have a SOC 2 report"
- ❌ "SOC 2 Type I report available"
- ❌ "SOC 2 attested"

**Reason:** SOC 2 examination and attestation require a formal examination by an independent CPA firm. MacTech Solutions has not undergone a SOC 2 examination.

---

### 3.3 Prohibited Compliance Claims

**DO NOT SAY:**
- ❌ "MacTech Solutions is compliant with SOC 2"
- ❌ "We comply with SOC 2"
- ❌ "SOC 2 compliant"
- ❌ "Compliant with SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria"
- ❌ "Meets SOC 2 requirements"

**Reason:** "Compliant with SOC 2" or "meets SOC 2 requirements" implies examination or attestation. Use "prepared for" or "designed to meet" instead.

---

### 3.4 Prohibited Report Claims

**DO NOT SAY:**
- ❌ "SOC 2 Type I report available"
- ❌ "We have a SOC 2 report"
- ❌ "SOC 2 examination report"
- ❌ "SOC 2 attestation report"

**Reason:** SOC 2 reports are issued by independent CPA firms after formal examination. MacTech Solutions has not undergone a SOC 2 examination and does not have a SOC 2 report.

---

### 3.5 Prohibited Examination Claims

**DO NOT SAY:**
- ❌ "SOC 2 examined"
- ❌ "SOC 2 Type I examined"
- ❌ "Examined for SOC 2"
- ❌ "Third-party SOC 2 examination"

**Reason:** SOC 2 examination requires formal examination by an independent CPA firm. MacTech Solutions has not undergone a SOC 2 examination.

## 4. Context-Specific Language

### 4.1 Enterprise Customer Proposals

**Recommended Language for Enterprise Proposals:**

"MacTech Solutions has completed an internal SOC 2 Type I readiness assessment that demonstrates our system design is prepared for a SOC 2 Type I examination. Our controls are designed to meet SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria (Security), addressing all 9 Common Criteria through our existing CMMC Level 2 implementation. SOC 2 Type I readiness documentation is available for review upon request."

**Rationale:** Emphasizes readiness and design alignment while maintaining accuracy.

**Supporting Documents:**
- SOC 2 Type I Readiness Overview
- Trust Services Criteria Mapping
- System Description

---

### 4.2 Prime Contractor Responses

**Recommended Language for Prime Contractor Security Reviews:**

"MacTech Solutions maintains CMMC 2.0 Level 2 compliance as our primary cybersecurity framework. Additionally, we have completed an internal SOC 2 Type I readiness assessment that demonstrates how our existing CMMC Level 2 controls align with SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria (Security). Our SOC 2 Type I Readiness Package documents design readiness across all 9 Common Criteria."

**Rationale:** Acknowledges CMMC as primary (required for DoD), with SOC 2 readiness as supplementary information.

**Supporting Documents:**
- CMMC Executive Attestation
- SOC 2 Type I Readiness Overview
- Trust Services Criteria Mapping

---

### 4.3 Internal Discussions

**Recommended Language for Internal Discussions:**

"MacTech Solutions has completed an internal SOC 2 Type I readiness assessment. Our system design, policies, and governance are prepared for a SOC 2 Type I examination. All 9 Common Criteria are addressed through existing CMMC Level 2 controls. SOC 2 readiness documentation is maintained and available for review."

**Rationale:** Internal language that accurately describes readiness status.

**Supporting Documents:**
- SOC 2 Type I Readiness Package (all documents)

---

### 4.4 RFP Responses

**Recommended Language for RFP Security Sections:**

"MacTech Solutions has completed an internal SOC 2 Type I readiness assessment that demonstrates our system design is prepared for a SOC 2 Type I examination. Our controls are designed to meet SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria (Security), addressing Common Criteria including Control Environment (CC1), Communication and Information (CC2), Risk Assessment (CC3), Monitoring Activities (CC4), Control Activities (CC5), Logical and Physical Access Controls (CC6), System Operations (CC7), Change Management (CC8), and Risk Mitigation (CC9). Our SOC 2 Type I readiness is based on our CMMC 2.0 Level 2 implementation."

**Rationale:** Provides specific Common Criteria details while maintaining accuracy and traceability.

**Supporting Documents:**
- SOC 2 Type I Readiness Overview
- Trust Services Criteria Mapping (for detailed criterion coverage)

---

### 4.5 Customer Security Questionnaires

**Recommended Language for Security Questionnaires:**

"MacTech Solutions has completed an internal SOC 2 Type I readiness assessment. Our system design is prepared for a SOC 2 Type I examination, with controls designed to meet SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria (Security). All 9 Common Criteria are addressed through our existing CMMC Level 2 implementation. SOC 2 Type I readiness documentation is available for review upon request."

**Rationale:** Concise, accurate, and offers documentation for verification.

**Supporting Documents:**
- SOC 2 Type I Readiness Overview (primary reference)
- Offer to provide additional documentation if requested

## 6. How to Answer SOC 2 Questions

### 6.1 "Are you SOC 2 certified?"

**Approved Response:**

"No, MacTech Solutions is not SOC 2 certified. SOC 2 does not have a certification program. However, we have completed an internal SOC 2 Type I readiness assessment that demonstrates our system design is prepared for a SOC 2 Type I examination. Our controls are designed to meet SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria (Security)."

---

### 6.2 "Do you have a SOC 2 report?"

**Approved Response:**

"We have not undergone a formal SOC 2 examination, so we do not have a SOC 2 report. However, we maintain internal SOC 2 Type I readiness documentation that demonstrates design readiness. Our readiness documentation is available for review."

---

### 6.3 "Are you SOC 2 compliant?"

**Approved Response:**

"MacTech Solutions does not claim SOC 2 compliance, as SOC 2 compliance requires a formal examination and attestation report. However, we have completed an internal SOC 2 Type I readiness assessment that demonstrates our system design is prepared for a SOC 2 Type I examination. Our controls are designed to meet SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria (Security)."

---

### 6.4 "Can you provide SOC 2 documentation?"

**Approved Response:**

"MacTech Solutions maintains SOC 2 Type I readiness documentation that demonstrates how our existing controls align with SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria (Security). The Readiness Package includes system description, risk assessment, Trust Services Criteria mapping, and readiness overview documentation. The documentation is available for review."

---

### 6.5 "How does SOC 2 relate to your CMMC compliance?"

**Approved Response:**

"MacTech Solutions' primary cybersecurity framework is CMMC 2.0 Level 2, which is required for DoD contracts. Our CMMC Level 2 implementation includes 110 NIST SP 800-171 controls. We have documented how these existing CMMC controls align with SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria (Security) in our SOC 2 Type I Readiness Package. This demonstrates that our CMMC implementation supports SOC 2 Common Criteria outcomes across all 9 criteria."

---

### 6.6 "What is your SOC 2 status?"

**Approved Response:**

"MacTech Solutions is not SOC 2 examined or attested. We have completed an internal SOC 2 Type I readiness assessment that demonstrates how our system design, policies, and governance are prepared for a SOC 2 Type I examination. Our readiness is based on existing CMMC Level 2 controls and is documented in our SOC 2 Type I Readiness Package."

## 7. Legal and Compliance Considerations

### 7.1 False Statements Law (18 U.S.C. § 1001)

**Critical Reminder:**

Knowingly and willfully making a false, fictitious, or fraudulent statement or representation in connection with federal contracts is a violation of 18 U.S.C. § 1001 and may result in:
- Criminal penalties including fines and imprisonment
- Civil penalties
- Contract termination
- Debarment from federal contracting

**All claims in this document are:**
- Truthful and accurate
- Supportable by existing evidence
- Conservative and defensible
- Reviewed for legal compliance

---

### 7.2 Accuracy Requirements

**Mandatory Requirements:**

1. **Truthfulness:** All claims must be truthful and accurate
2. **Evidence Support:** All claims must be supportable by existing evidence
3. **No Speculation:** Do not make claims about future capabilities or planned improvements
4. **No Overstatement:** Use only approved language; do not embellish or exaggerate
5. **Documentation:** Maintain documentation supporting all claims

---

### 7.3 Review Process

**Before Using SOC 2 Claims:**

1. Review this document for approved language
2. Verify that supporting evidence exists
3. Ensure claims are appropriate for the context
4. Consult compliance team if uncertain
5. Document any deviations (which should be rare)
